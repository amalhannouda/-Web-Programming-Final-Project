Maghe Sankranti Festival

Maghe Sankranti festival or Makar Sankranti festival is one of the main festivals celebrated in the month of Maghe (according to the Bikram Sambat calendar, around January 14). The festival is considered the start of warmer days compared to the cold month of Poush (December). It is believed that from this day the sun begins to move towards the northern hemisphere. It is a solstice festival celebrated in Nepal. During the festival, Nepalese people perform their cultural dances, dress in traditional Tharu clothes, eat and drink with their relatives.

Maghe Sankranti is considered to mark the beginning of an auspicious phase in Nepalese culture. It is cited as the “sacred phase of transition”. The festival is marked by the consumption of ghee and chaku which are believed to be good for body heat. Others also enjoy yams, sweet potatoes, fried fish, and black and white sesame balls.
