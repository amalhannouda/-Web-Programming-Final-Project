Dashain and Tihar Festivals

Dashain and Tihar are two of the greatest festivals of Nepal. According to Hindus mythology, Dashain is a celebration of the victory of the goddess Durga over the evil Mahishasura. while Tihar is a celebration of lights and colors dedicated to the god of wealth and prosperity Laxmi. Although these festivals are linked to a particular religion, all Nepali people celebrate Dashain and Tihar with great joy and excitement.

Both of these festivals fall during the fall season in the month of Ashwin (September- October) and are considered the longest festival holidays. Nepal people celebrate Dashain- also called Vijaya Dashami- for 10 days by spending time with their family and friends, eating delicious foods and receiving tika and blessings from the elderly. Just after Dashain, Tihar is celebrated for five days. It is the festival on which Nepalese people worship dogs for their loyalty, offering them garlands and good food.
