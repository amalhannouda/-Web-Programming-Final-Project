Indra Jatra festival

Indra Jatra festival, one of the biggest festivals celebrated in the Kathmandu Valley, marks the start of a month-long autumn festival season. Indra Jatra is celebrated 15th day of Yenla, which is the 11th month of the Nepalese calendar. The eight-day Indra Jatra festival falls in September and is one of the most exciting and revered festivals of the Newar community in the Kathmandu Valley.

In this festival, farmers pay homage to Lord Indra and his mother Dagini and pray for their harvest season to be blessed. the festival is masked by dances, religious texts, lights and colors and the living goddess Kumari. For the mast-raising ceremony, hundreds of spectators gather in Palace Square and the surrounding temples. The chariot of Kumari, the living goddess, is taken in procession through the main streets of Kathmandu.
