Maha Shivaratri Festival

Maha Shivaratri Festival is the most important event in the Hindu spiritual calendar. During this annual festival, devotees glorify and revere the life of Lord Shiva. They worship lingam (symbol of Shiva), observe night fast and meditate on several ethics during the festival. It is celebrated on the fourteenth day of the waning moon in the Nepali month of Falgun (February/March). During this day, the Pashupatinath Temple in Kathmandu witnesses a huge influx of devotees and Sadhus (holy sages) offering their worship. Married women in Nepal pray for the welfare of their husbands. However, unmarried girls offer their worship to find a husband like Lord Shiva who is considered as the ideal husband.

Unlike most Hindu festivals, Maha Shivaratri is not just an obviously joyful festival to feast on. On the contrary, he sees the idea of ​​self-reflection and personal growth leaving behind everything that hinders our success.

Here are some ways devotees celebrate Maha Shivaratri:

  • They practice nocturnal fasting and observe a vigil contemplating spirituality.
  • They visit Shiva temples and sacred rivers to offer their worship to the Lord.
  • Devotees meditate on several ethics, chant mantras, sing songs and listen to stories of Lord Shiva.
  • They participate in Rudra Puja or Mahashivratri Puja to honor Lord Shiva.
  • People worship Shiva Lingam by offering Bel Patra (leaves of the beautiful tree), milk, sandalwood paste, white colored flowers and Dhaturo (a type of intoxicating plant).
  • Devotees often donate food and clothing to the poor and needy at the end of the Maha Shivratri puja.
