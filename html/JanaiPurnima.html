Janai Purnima Festival

Janai Purnima One of these festivals that is celebrated by nearly every Nepalese family, also known as the Sacred Thread Festival. This festival is celebreted on the full moon day of the Shrawan or Bhadra month, according to the Nepali calendar. Janai Purnima is one of the most sacred and important festivals in Hinduism. The festival symbolizes the connection of purity and security. Purnima is the full moon, and Janai means the sacred thread. The annual practice of Changing Janai is performed by Hindu Nepali males on this day. Janai is a sacred cotton thread worn on the chest by Hindu men, and worn only by men who have completed the Pratapandhan religious rituals. Priests give individuals who do not wear Janai a special colored thread called a "Doro" to put around their wrist. This sacred thread called Doro is worn for safety and protection, according to belief.
